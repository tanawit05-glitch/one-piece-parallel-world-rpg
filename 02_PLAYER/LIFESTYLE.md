# LIFESTYLE SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Lifestyle System defines how a character lives between major adventures.

Lifestyle represents daily routines, living standards, habits, personal comfort, and resource management.

It provides continuity between important story events and helps create a believable living world.

---

# Design Philosophy

Characters do not exist only during dramatic moments.

They eat.

Sleep.

Work.

Relax.

Study.

Train.

Socialize.

Recover.

Daily life shapes long-term development as much as extraordinary adventures.

---

# Lifestyle Definition

Lifestyle represents the overall quality and pattern of a character's everyday life.

It includes:

- Living Conditions
- Daily Routine
- Personal Habits
- Financial Stability
- Nutrition
- Hygiene
- Rest
- Recreation
- Education
- Social Activities

Lifestyle is descriptive rather than numerical.

---

# Living Conditions

A character's living conditions depend upon their current circumstances.

Examples include:

Temporary Camps.

Small Apartments.

Village Homes.

Crew Cabins.

Marine Barracks.

Luxury Estates.

Inns.

Ships.

Hidden Bases.

Living conditions naturally change throughout the campaign.

---

# Daily Routine

Most characters develop routines.

Examples:

Morning Training.

Medical Practice.

Cooking.

Fishing.

Navigation Study.

Ship Maintenance.

Trading.

Patrolling.

Routine creates consistency within the world.

Unexpected events may interrupt daily life.

---

# Rest

Adequate rest is essential.

Rest supports:

Physical Recovery.

Mental Recovery.

Medical Healing.

Long-term Performance.

Insufficient rest may gradually reduce effectiveness until proper recovery occurs.

The Medical System determines specific recovery effects.

---

# Nutrition

Food influences health and morale.

A balanced diet supports long-term wellbeing.

Poor nutrition may gradually affect:

Energy.

Recovery.

Physical condition.

Professional performance.

The Cooking System and Economy System determine food availability.

---

# Hygiene

Cleanliness influences both health and social perception.

Examples:

Medical environments require high standards.

Long voyages make hygiene difficult.

Luxury environments provide greater comfort.

Poor hygiene may influence NPC reactions and increase medical risks.

---

# Personal Habits

Characters naturally develop habits.

Examples:

Reading before sleep.

Morning exercise.

Collecting maps.

Writing journals.

Practicing instruments.

Drinking coffee.

Smoking.

Meditation.

Habits provide roleplaying opportunities and strengthen character identity.

---

# Recreation

People require leisure.

Examples:

Music.

Games.

Storytelling.

Fishing.

Art.

Exercise.

Festivals.

Conversation.

Healthy recreation improves morale and strengthens relationships.

---

# Education

Characters continue learning outside major adventures.

Examples:

Reading books.

Studying charts.

Learning languages.

Practicing medicine.

Research.

Education supports believable long-term development.

---

# Financial Lifestyle

Income influences daily living standards.

Possible lifestyles include:

Modest.

Comfortable.

Prosperous.

Luxurious.

Wealthy characters may enjoy greater comfort,

but also greater responsibility.

Lifestyle naturally reflects financial reality.

---

# Responsibilities

Many characters possess ongoing responsibilities.

Examples:

Managing a business.

Supporting family.

Leading a crew.

Maintaining a ship.

Serving the Marines.

Teaching students.

Responsibilities continue even when the main story moves elsewhere.

---

# Lifestyle Changes

Lifestyle evolves naturally.

Examples:

Joining a pirate crew.

Receiving a promotion.

Buying a ship.

Opening a clinic.

Becoming homeless.

Retiring.

Major life events often transform daily routines.

---

# Lifestyle and Occupation

Occupation strongly influences Lifestyle.

Doctors spend time treating patients.

Merchants travel.

Shipwrights repair vessels.

Marines patrol.

Farmers follow seasonal work.

Lifestyle should always reflect professional responsibilities.

---

# Lifestyle and Relationships

Daily life strengthens relationships.

Shared meals.

Shared work.

Conversations.

Training.

Celebrations.

Ordinary moments often build stronger bonds than heroic victories.

---

# Lifestyle and Reputation

Consistent behavior influences public opinion.

Examples:

A generous doctor.

A hardworking merchant.

A disciplined Marine.

A careless captain.

Reputation develops through repeated observation.

---

# Lifestyle and Character Growth

Lifestyle influences long-term development.

Daily discipline often determines future success.

Small habits become lasting strengths.

Neglected responsibilities create future problems.

Growth is shaped by ordinary life as much as extraordinary events.

---

# NPC Lifestyle

NPCs possess independent daily lives.

Shopkeepers open businesses.

Children attend school.

Doctors visit patients.

Marines perform patrols.

Merchants travel between islands.

The world continues functioning even when players are absent.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

The Game Master should use Lifestyle to:

Create immersion.

Develop relationships.

Introduce local events.

Generate personal stories.

Present meaningful downtime activities.

Daily life should feel valuable,

not like empty time between adventures.

---

# Future Expansion

Future systems may expand Lifestyle through:

Housing.

Businesses.

Property Ownership.

Family Life.

Crew Management.

Political Responsibilities.

All additions must remain compatible with the Core Lifestyle System.

---

# Engine Principles

The Lifestyle System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

A character's life is defined not only by great adventures,

but also by the ordinary days between them.
