# TRAIT SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Trait System defines the enduring positive characteristics that shape a character's behavior, decision-making, and interactions with the world.

Traits represent tendencies rather than abilities.

They describe who a character is, not what a character can do.

Traits provide narrative identity while supporting believable roleplaying throughout the campaign.

---

# Design Philosophy

Two characters with identical Attributes and Skills should still feel completely different.

Traits create individuality.

They influence:

- Personality
- Decision Making
- Habits
- Emotional Responses
- Social Interactions
- Leadership Style
- Problem Solving

Traits never remove player agency.

The player always chooses how their character acts.

Traits simply establish what comes naturally.

---

# Trait Definition

A Trait is a persistent positive characteristic.

Traits are not temporary emotions.

Traits are not supernatural powers.

Traits are not Skills.

Traits describe consistent patterns of behavior.

---

# Character Creation

Every playable character begins with one or more Traits during Character Creation.

Traits should logically match:

- Background
- Childhood
- Education
- Occupation
- Personal History

Traits should never be selected solely for mechanical advantage.

---

# Narrative Role

Traits help answer questions such as:

How does this character react under pressure?

How do they treat strangers?

What kind of leader are they?

What habits define them?

How do other people remember them?

# Narrative Influence

Traits influence storytelling by affecting:

NPC expectations.

Dialogue.

Reputation.

Trust.

First impressions.

Long-term relationships.

Traits do not force specific outcomes.

The Game Master interprets their influence naturally.

---

# Consistency

Players are encouraged to roleplay consistently with their chosen Traits.

Occasional exceptions are normal.

Persistent contradictions should be explained through character development.

---

# Trait Synergy

Traits naturally interact with other systems.

Examples:

Calm

↓

Better emotional control during negotiations.

Observant

↓

More believable investigative behavior.

Compassionate

↓

Stronger patient relationships.

Disciplined

↓

More effective long-term training.

Trait synergy should always remain narrative first.

---

# Trait Development

Traits are generally stable.

However,

Major life events may gradually strengthen,

weaken,

or transform them.

Examples:

A fearless sailor becoming cautious after disaster.

A selfish merchant learning generosity.

A quiet recruit becoming an inspiring captain.

Such changes should occur through meaningful narrative progression.

---

# Replacing Traits

Replacing a Trait should be rare.

Requirements include:

Major character development.

Long-term story progression.

Significant emotional events.

Game Master approval.

Traits should never change simply to gain mechanical advantage.

---

# Hidden Traits

NPCs possess their own Traits.

Players discover them through:

Conversation.

Observation.

Shared experiences.

Reputation.

No character immediately knows another person's true nature.

---

# Trait Conflicts

Characters may possess Traits that create internal tension.

Examples:

Brave

+

Patient

Curious

+

Cautious

Kind

+

Strict

Logical

+

Compassionate

Conflicting Traits produce interesting roleplaying opportunities.

---

# Trait and Skill

Traits never replace Skills.

Example:

Being Curious does not grant Investigation.

Being Brave does not improve Swordsmanship.

Being Organized does not create Engineering knowledge.

Traits influence behavior.

Skills determine capability.

---

# Trait and Attribute

Traits are independent of Attributes.

A physically weak character may still be Courageous.

A highly intelligent character may still be Humble.

A charismatic leader may still be Patient.

The systems remain separate.

---

# Trait and Reputation

Consistently demonstrated Traits gradually shape Reputation.

Examples:

Reliable workers become trusted.

Honest merchants gain loyal customers.

Compassionate doctors become respected.

Cruel captains become feared.

Reputation reflects actions,

not Trait selection alone.

---

# Trait and Relationships

Traits strongly influence long-term relationships.

NPCs remember patterns.

Repeated demonstrations of kindness,

discipline,

or fairness affect future interactions.

Trust develops through consistency.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

Traits should be referenced naturally.

The Game Master should never force players to act according to their Traits.

Instead,

Traits provide context for:

NPC reactions.

Story opportunities.

Relationship development.

Personal growth.

---

# Future Expansion

Future systems may introduce:

Cultural Traits.

Regional Traits.

Occupational Traits.

Faction Traits.

Inherited Family Traits.

All additions must remain compatible with the Core Trait System.

---

# Engine Principles

The Trait System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

Traits define the character's nature.

Actions define the character's legacy.
