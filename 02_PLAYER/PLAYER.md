# PLAYER SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Player System defines how a player exists inside the Narrative Simulation Engine.

A player controls one Character.

The player influences only the decisions of that character.

The player never controls:

- NPCs
- World Events
- Politics
- Other Crews
- Time
- Random Outcomes

The world exists independently of the player.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Player is a participant inside a living world.

The engine does not adapt itself around players.

Players must adapt to the world.

Every action has consequences.

Every choice creates new possibilities while closing others.

---

# Player Authority

Players may decide:

- Movement
- Speech
- Combat Decisions
- Tactical Choices
- Skill Usage
- Equipment Usage
- Social Interactions
- Long-Term Goals
- Crew Discussions

Players may not decide:

- Hidden Information
- NPC Intentions
- Dice Results
- World Events
- Secret Systems
- Weather
- Political Decisions
- Enemy Strategies

---

# Character Ownership

Each player permanently owns one playable character unless:

- Character Death
- Retirement
- Story Exit
- Character Transfer approved by the Game Master

Ownership includes:

- Personality
- Decisions
- Personal Goals
- Relationships
- Growth

Ownership does not include immunity from consequences.

---

# Player Knowledge vs Character Knowledge

The engine always separates:

## Player Knowledge

Information known by the human player.

## Character Knowledge

Information the character has actually learned.

The Game Master always resolves actions using Character Knowledge.

Metagaming is not permitted.

---

# Roleplaying Standard

Players should make decisions according to:

- Personality
- Values
- Knowledge
- Emotional State
- Current Situation

Optimal decisions are not required.

Believable decisions are preferred.

---

# Player Intent

Before resolving uncertain actions the Game Master determines:

Intent

Examples:

"I want to scare him."

"I want to escape."

"I want to protect civilians."

"I want to gather information."

Intent determines:

- Dice
- Difficulty
- Possible Outcomes
- Narrative Consequences

---

# Decision Before Roll

The engine follows this sequence:

Intent

↓

Approach

↓

Difficulty

↓

Roll

↓

Narrative Result

Players never roll before declaring intent.

---

# Failure Philosophy

Failure is content.

Failure creates:

- New Problems
- New Opportunities
- Character Growth
- Story Development

Failure should rarely stop progression completely.

---

# Player Freedom

Players may attempt any reasonable action.

The engine does not rely on predefined action lists.

Creativity is encouraged.

The Game Master determines:

- Feasibility
- Difficulty
- Consequences

---

# Risk and Consequences

Every meaningful action carries risk.

Possible consequences include:

- Injury
- Reputation Changes
- Legal Trouble
- Political Effects
- Resource Loss
- Crew Conflict
- Enemy Attention

---

# Cooperation

The engine rewards teamwork.

Examples:

- Combined Skills
- Tactical Coordination
- Shared Planning
- Emotional Support
- Medical Assistance
- Navigation Cooperation

---

# Player Responsibility

Players are responsible for:

- Roleplaying honestly
- Respecting world logic
- Accepting consequences
- Avoiding metagaming
- Supporting collaborative storytelling

---

# Game Master Responsibility

The Game Master must:

- Remain impartial
- Apply rules consistently
- Protect narrative fairness
- Respect player agency
- Never force predetermined outcomes

---

# Character Replacement

If a playable character permanently exits the story:

1. Create a new character.

2. Introduce the character naturally.

3. Preserve world continuity.

4. Previous consequences remain part of the world.

---

# Engine Principles

The Player System always follows:

- Story Before System
- Narrative Simulation
- Hidden But Fair
- World Before Player
- No Plot Armor

The player experiences the world.

The world never exists solely for the player.
