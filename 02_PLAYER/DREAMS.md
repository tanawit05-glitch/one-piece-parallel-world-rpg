# DREAM SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Dream System defines the ultimate aspiration that drives a character throughout their life.

A Dream represents the highest goal a character wishes to achieve.

It is the emotional and philosophical foundation of the character's journey.

Dreams give purpose to adventure.

Without a Dream,

a character merely reacts to the world.

With a Dream,

a character actively shapes their own future.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every great journey begins with a reason.

Power.

Wealth.

Knowledge.

Freedom.

Justice.

Family.

Adventure.

Discovery.

Each Dream represents something the character values above ordinary life.

Dreams are not objectives assigned by the Game Master.

They belong entirely to the character.

---

# Dream Definition

A Dream is:

- Long-term
- Personal
- Meaningful
- Difficult
- Inspirational

Dreams should require years of effort.

Some Dreams may never be fully completed.

That possibility makes them meaningful.

---

# Character Creation

Every playable character begins with one Primary Dream.

The Dream is selected during Character Creation.

It should naturally fit the character's:

- Background
- Values
- Personality
- Occupation
- Life Experiences

Dreams should never be selected for mechanical advantages.

---

# Characteristics of a Good Dream

A meaningful Dream is:

Specific.

Believable.

Motivating.

Narratively interesting.

Capable of creating difficult choices.

Examples:

Become the greatest shipwright in the world.

Discover every island in the Grand Line.

Develop a cure for every known disease.

Protect every village in my homeland.

Build a fleet capable of crossing any sea.

Create the world's finest restaurant.

Reveal the truth behind forgotten history.

---

# Poorly Defined Dreams

Dreams should avoid being:

Too vague.

Too easy.

Entirely dependent upon another character.

Purely mechanical.

Examples:

Become stronger.

Earn money.

Win battles.

Travel somewhere.

These goals may support a Dream,

but should not replace one.

---

# Personal Motivation

A Dream should answer:

Why does this matter?

Personal motivation creates emotional investment.

Different characters may share the same Dream,

yet pursue it for entirely different reasons.

---

# Dream and Roleplaying

Dreams influence:

Decision Making.

Risk Assessment.

Relationships.

Leadership.

Personal Sacrifice.

Long-Term Planning.

The player remains free to ignore their Dream.

Doing so becomes part of the character's story.

---

# Dream Conflicts

Dreams may conflict with:

Other Dreams.

Crew Goals.

Faction Objectives.

Political Events.

Personal Relationships.

Survival.

These conflicts create compelling narratives.

---

# Shared Dreams

Some groups may eventually share a common Dream.

Examples:

Building a legendary crew.

Creating a peaceful nation.

Exploring every sea.

Protecting civilians.

Shared Dreams strengthen group identity without replacing individual Dreams.

---

# Impossible Dreams

A Dream may appear impossible.

This does not make it invalid.

Many extraordinary achievements begin as impossible ambitions.

The world should challenge Dreams,

not dismiss them.

---

# Dream Evolution

Dreams may evolve naturally.

Examples:

A doctor seeking fame learns to value saving lives.

A pirate chasing treasure discovers freedom matters more.

A merchant pursuing wealth begins rebuilding devastated communities.

The core identity of the Dream should remain recognizable.

---

# Dream Completion

Some Dreams can eventually be fulfilled.

Completion should feel significant.

Possible outcomes include:

Retirement.

New Dream.

Legacy.

Teaching others.

Leadership.

Dream completion represents transformation,

not the end of meaningful play.

---

# Broken Dreams

Dreams sometimes fail.

Failure may occur because of:

Personal choices.

World events.

Loss.

Sacrifice.

Time.

Broken Dreams create powerful emotional stories.

Characters may:

Accept.

Adapt.

Replace.

Fight harder.

The choice belongs to the player.

---

# Hidden Dreams

NPCs possess Dreams of their own.

Many Dreams remain hidden.

Players discover them through:

Conversation.

Observation.

Trust.

Shared experiences.

Dreams explain why NPCs make difficult choices.

---

# Dreams and Relationships

Characters with compatible Dreams often become allies.

Conflicting Dreams may create rivals,

even between friends.

Relationships are strengthened or weakened through repeated interactions involving each character's aspirations.

---

# Dreams and Reputation

Remarkable dedication to a Dream naturally influences Reputation.

Examples:

A doctor known for treating anyone regardless of status.

A navigator obsessed with charting unknown waters.

A shipwright rebuilding ships after disasters.

The world remembers extraordinary commitment.

---

# Dreams and Character Growth

Dreams guide long-term personal development.

Characters often acquire:

New Skills.

New Relationships.

New Opportunities.

New Responsibilities.

Dreams provide direction,

not predetermined outcomes.

---

# Dreams and the Living World

The world reacts to determined individuals.

As characters pursue their Dreams,

the world changes around them.

New allies appear.

New enemies emerge.

Political interests shift.

Rumors spread.

Dreams become part of world history.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

The Game Master should use Dreams to:

Create meaningful story hooks.

Present moral dilemmas.

Offer difficult opportunities.

Introduce long-term rivals.

Reward dedication.

Challenge personal beliefs.

Dreams should shape the narrative naturally,

never through forced events.

---

# Future Expansion

Future systems may introduce:

Crew Dreams.

Faction Dreams.

National Dreams.

Inherited Family Dreams.

Ideological Dreams.

Every expansion must remain compatible with the Core Dream System.

---

# Engine Principles

The Dream System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

Dreams do not guarantee success.

They give characters a reason to keep moving forward despite failure.
