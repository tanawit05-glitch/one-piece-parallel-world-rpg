# FLAW SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Flaw System defines the persistent weaknesses, limitations, and personal shortcomings that shape a character throughout the Narrative Simulation Engine.

Every playable character possesses flaws.

No individual is without weakness.

Flaws create believable characters, meaningful conflicts, and opportunities for growth.

---

# Design Philosophy

Perfection does not exist.

Every strength creates limitations.

Every virtue has a cost.

Flaws are not punishments.

They are narrative tools that create drama, tension, difficult decisions, and personal growth.

A memorable character is defined as much by their weaknesses as by their strengths.

---

# Flaw Definition

A Flaw is a persistent characteristic that regularly creates challenges for the character.

Flaws are:

- Personal
- Consistent
- Believable
- Imperfect
- Narratively meaningful

Flaws are not:

- Temporary emotions
- Injuries
- Diseases
- Status Effects
- Lack of Skill

---

# Character Creation

Every playable character must possess at least one meaningful Flaw.

Additional Flaws are allowed when supported by the character concept.

Flaws should naturally arise from:

- Background
- Childhood
- Trauma
- Education
- Occupation
- Personal History

---

# Narrative Purpose

Flaws exist to create:

- Internal conflict
- Difficult choices
- Personal struggle
- Relationship tension
- Character development
- Story opportunities

The purpose of a Flaw is never to punish the player.

# Narrative Influence

Flaws naturally influence:

Decision Making.

Relationships.

Leadership.

Reputation.

Stress Responses.

Professional Conduct.

The Game Master interprets these influences through narrative.

---

# Player Agency

Players always retain control of their characters.

A Flaw never forces an action.

Instead,

it suggests believable tendencies.

The player may choose to resist a Flaw,

accept it,

or struggle against it.

That struggle creates compelling stories.

---

# Internal Conflict

Characters often possess Traits and Flaws that oppose one another.

Examples:

Brave

+

Impulsive

Kind

+

Overprotective

Analytical

+

Perfectionist

Confident

+

Arrogant

These conflicts create believable personalities.

---

# Consequences

Ignoring a Flaw may have no consequence.

Following a Flaw may create:

Complications.

New Opportunities.

Relationship Changes.

Unexpected Problems.

Interesting Stories.

The Game Master should prefer consequences over penalties.

---

# Flaw Evolution

Flaws may evolve through long-term character development.

Possible outcomes include:

Acceptance.

Improvement.

Transformation.

Replacement.

Worsening.

Development must always occur through narrative events,

never through automatic advancement.

---

# Replacing a Flaw

Replacing a Flaw requires:

Major personal growth.

Long-term roleplaying.

Meaningful story events.

Game Master approval.

The original Flaw remains part of the character's history.

---

# Hidden Flaws

NPCs possess their own Flaws.

Players discover them through:

Observation.

Conversation.

Rumors.

Shared experiences.

No individual fully understands another person at first meeting.

---

# Relationships

Flaws strongly influence long-term relationships.

Examples:

A stubborn captain may frustrate the crew.

An arrogant doctor may lose patient trust.

A jealous rival may become dangerous.

A naive merchant may attract scammers.

Relationships evolve naturally through repeated interaction.

---

# Reputation

Repeated demonstrations of a Flaw may influence Reputation.

Examples:

Greed.

Cowardice.

Cruelty.

Dishonesty.

Impulsiveness.

Reputation reflects observed behavior,

not hidden personality.

---

# Flaw and Skills

Flaws never directly reduce Skill Ratings.

Instead,

they influence how Skills are used.

Example:

An impulsive surgeon may rush procedures.

A perfectionist shipwright may work more slowly.

An arrogant negotiator may underestimate others.

The Skill remains unchanged.

Behavior changes.

---

# Flaw and Attributes

Flaws are independent from Attributes.

A powerful warrior may still be insecure.

A brilliant scientist may be arrogant.

A charismatic leader may be selfish.

Each system serves a different purpose.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

The Game Master should use Flaws to:

Create believable complications.

Generate emotional conflict.

Encourage roleplaying.

Develop long-term stories.

Never use Flaws solely as punishment.

They exist to enrich the narrative.

---

# Future Expansion

Future systems may introduce:

Cultural Flaws.

Occupational Flaws.

Faction-Based Flaws.

Inherited Family Flaws.

Psychological Archetypes.

All future additions must remain compatible with the Core Flaw System.

---

# Engine Principles

The Flaw System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

Weakness does not diminish a character.

It makes their victories meaningful.
