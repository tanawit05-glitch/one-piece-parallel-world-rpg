# BACKGROUND SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Background System defines a character's life before becoming an active participant in the campaign.

A Background explains who the character was before the story begins.

It establishes personal history, education, social standing, early experiences, relationships, opportunities, and worldview.

Backgrounds provide narrative context rather than predetermined destiny.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every individual has a past.

That past shapes:

- Knowledge
- Values
- Habits
- Relationships
- Opportunities
- Fears
- Strengths
- Weaknesses

Backgrounds explain where a character came from.

They do not determine where the character will go.

---

# Narrative Role

Backgrounds exist to answer fundamental questions.

Examples:

Who raised this character?

Where did they grow up?

How were they educated?

What major events shaped them?

Why did they leave home?

What do they believe?

Who remembers them?

The answers create narrative consistency throughout the campaign.

---

# Background Creation

Every playable character must possess one primary Background.

The Background is selected during Character Creation.

The chosen Background becomes a permanent part of the character's history.

---

# Background Components

Every Background contains the following elements.

- Origin
- Childhood
- Education
- Family
- Community
- Early Occupation
- Significant Event
- Starting Relationships
- Starting Reputation
- Personal Values

Each component contributes to the character's identity.

---

# Origin

Origin identifies where the character comes from.

Examples include:

- East Blue
- South Blue
- North Blue
- West Blue
- Paradise
- New World
- Sky Island
- Calm Belt Settlement
- Remote Island

Origin influences cultural familiarity but does not grant mechanical bonuses.

---

# Childhood

Childhood defines the character's early environment.

Examples include:

- Fishing Village
- Busy Port
- Noble Estate
- Marine Base
- Merchant Family
- Traveling Circus
- Mountain Settlement
- Shipyard
- Religious Community

Childhood shapes perspective rather than ability.

---

# Education

Education represents formal and informal learning before the campaign.

Possible sources include:

- Family
- Apprenticeship
- Marine Academy
- Private Tutor
- Self-Education
- Religious Institution
- Local School
- Practical Experience

Education supports believable Skill selection.

---

# Family

Family relationships provide narrative anchors.

Family may include:

- Parents
- Grandparents
- Siblings
- Adoptive Family
- Guardians
- Extended Relatives

Family members remain part of the living world unless otherwise established.

---

# Community

Every character belonged to a community before becoming an adventurer.

Communities influence:

- Reputation
- Customs
- Expectations
- Local Knowledge
- Personal Connections

Communities continue to evolve even after the character leaves.

---

# Early Occupation

Many characters worked before the campaign.

Examples include:

- Fisher
- Doctor
- Carpenter
- Hunter
- Merchant
- Sailor
- Guard
- Farmer
- Blacksmith

Occupation is determined separately within the Occupation System.

Background explains why that occupation was learned.

---

# Significant Event

Every Background contains one defining life event.

Examples:

- Natural disaster
- Pirate attack
- Marine rescue
- Family tragedy
- Great discovery
- Shipwreck
- Political conflict
- Disease outbreak

The event explains why the character's life changed.

---

# Values

Background influences personal values.

Examples:

- Justice
- Freedom
- Wealth
- Honor
- Knowledge
- Family
- Duty
- Adventure

Values influence roleplaying but never remove player agency.

---

# Starting Knowledge

Characters begin with knowledge appropriate to their Background.

Examples:

A fisherman understands local waters.

A doctor recognizes common diseases.

A merchant understands trade customs.

A navigator understands basic charts.

Background knowledge should always remain believable.

---

# Cultural Familiarity

Characters are naturally familiar with the culture in which they were raised.

This familiarity may include:

- Customs
- Dialects
- Food
- Festivals
- Superstitions
- Local Laws

Characters may misunderstand unfamiliar cultures.

---

# Starting Relationships

Background establishes existing relationships.

Examples:

Friends

Teachers

Employers

Family

Childhood Rivals

Former Shipmates

These individuals remain active members of the world.

---

# Starting Reputation

Most characters begin as ordinary people.

Some Backgrounds may provide:

Positive reputation

Negative reputation

Professional recognition

Family reputation

Local notoriety

Reputation never guarantees future treatment.

NPCs judge characters based on current behavior as well as reputation.

---

# Resources

Background may explain access to:

Tools

Books

Property

Family Business

Professional Contacts

Safe Locations

These resources belong to the story rather than the player.

The Game Master determines whether they remain accessible.

---

# Background Secrets

Some Backgrounds contain hidden information.

Examples:

Unknown heritage

Hidden inheritance

Forgotten promises

False identity

Government records

Secret organizations

Players may know or may not know these secrets depending upon the campaign.

The Game Master determines disclosure.

---

# Background Conflicts

A Background should create unresolved narrative opportunities.

Examples:

Missing family member

Debt

Broken friendship

Former mentor

Failed promise

Old enemy

These conflicts generate future stories.

---

# Changing Background

A character's Background never changes.

However,

Its meaning may change as new information is revealed.

Characters discover new truths about their own past throughout the campaign.

---

# Interaction With Other Systems

Background interacts with:

Character Creation

Occupation

Skills

Relationships

Reputation

Dreams

Ambitions

Lifestyle

NPC System

Faction System

World System

Every interaction must remain logically consistent.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

The Game Master should use Backgrounds to:

Introduce story hooks.

Create believable NPC connections.

Generate emotional investment.

Reward thoughtful character creation.

Expand the living world.

Background should never be ignored after Session 0.

---

# Engine Principles

The Background System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

Every character begins the story with a past.

That past continues to shape the future long after the adventure begins.
