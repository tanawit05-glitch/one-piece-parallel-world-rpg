# ATTRIBUTE SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Attribute System defines the innate physical and mental capabilities of every character within the Narrative Simulation Engine.

Attributes represent natural potential rather than learned expertise.

They serve as the foundation for all character actions and interact with Skills, Equipment, Haki, Devil Fruits, Status Effects, and other gameplay systems.

Attributes rarely change over a character's lifetime.

Growth is primarily represented through Skills, Experience, and Narrative Development.

---

# Design Philosophy

Attributes describe what a character is naturally capable of.

Skills describe what a character has learned.

Equipment describes what a character possesses.

Power Systems describe extraordinary abilities.

These systems are intentionally separated.

---

# Core Attribute Model

Every playable character possesses six Core Attributes.

Each attribute represents a broad area of capability rather than a specific statistic.

The six Core Attributes are:

- Strength
- Agility
- Endurance
- Intelligence
- Awareness
- Presence

No additional base attributes exist.

---

# Strength

Strength represents raw physical power.

Strength influences:

- Lifting
- Grappling
- Climbing
- Breaking Objects
- Melee Force
- Physical Labor

Strength does not determine accuracy.

---

# Agility

Agility represents speed, coordination, precision, and balance.

Agility influences:

- Dodging
- Acrobatics
- Stealth Movement
- Balance
- Reflexes
- Fine Motor Control

Agility does not determine endurance.

---

# Endurance

Endurance represents physical resilience.

Endurance influences:

- Stamina
- Fatigue Resistance
- Injury Resistance
- Long-distance Travel
- Survival
- Physical Recovery

Endurance is not equivalent to health points.

---

# Intelligence

Intelligence represents reasoning and accumulated knowledge.

Intelligence influences:

- Investigation
- Learning
- Medicine
- Engineering
- Navigation Theory
- Tactical Planning

Intelligence does not represent wisdom or perception.

---

# Awareness

Awareness represents perception and environmental understanding.

Awareness influences:

- Observation
- Detecting Danger
- Tracking
- Reading Situations
- Searching
- Instinctive Reactions

Awareness supports but does not replace Observation Skills.

---

# Presence

Presence represents force of personality.

Presence influences:

- Leadership
- Persuasion
- Negotiation
- Intimidation
- Inspiration
- Social Confidence

Presence does not guarantee success in conversations.

NPC reactions always depend on context.

---

# Attribute Scale

All Core Attributes use the same rating scale.

| Rating | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Extremely Poor |
| 2 | Poor |
| 3 | Below Average |
| 4 | Average |
| 5 | Above Average |
| 6 | Excellent |
| 7 | Exceptional |
| 8 | Elite |
| 9 | Legendary |
| 10 | World-Class |

Ratings above 10 are not naturally attainable.

---

# Starting Limits

New player characters begin within the limits defined by the Character Creation System.

Characters cannot begin as legendary individuals.

Initial attribute distribution must remain balanced.

The exact allocation method is defined by the Character Creation rules.

---

# Attribute Checks

Whenever an action relies primarily on natural ability, the Game Master determines the most appropriate Attribute.

Examples:

Breaking a locked door

→ Strength

Balancing on a narrow mast during a storm

→ Agility

Marching for several days without rest

→ Endurance

Diagnosing an unfamiliar disease

→ Intelligence

Spotting an ambush before it begins

→ Awareness

Negotiating with a suspicious merchant

→ Presence

---

# Combined Checks

Many actions require both Attributes and Skills.

Examples:

Swordsmanship

→ Agility + Weapon Skill

Emergency Surgery

→ Intelligence + Medical Skill

Ocean Navigation

→ Intelligence + Navigation Skill

Tracking a Fugitive

→ Awareness + Survival Skill

Public Speech

→ Presence + Leadership Skill

The Game Master determines the most appropriate combination.

---

# Attribute Contest

When two characters oppose each other using natural ability, both sides make an appropriate Attribute Check.

Examples:

Arm Wrestling

Strength vs Strength

Chasing on Rooftops

Agility vs Agility

Staring Contest

Presence vs Presence

Holding Breath Underwater

Endurance vs Endurance

The higher successful result gains the advantage.

---

# Attribute Limitations

High Attributes do not guarantee success.

Environmental conditions, injuries, fatigue, fear, equipment, weather, and narrative circumstances may all influence outcomes.

The world remains the primary authority.

---

# Temporary Modifiers

Attributes are normally permanent.

Temporary effects may modify performance without permanently changing the Attribute.

Examples:

- Exhaustion
- Injury
- Poison
- Hunger
- Weather
- Motivation
- Fear

Temporary modifiers expire when their source is resolved.

---

# Permanent Changes

Permanent Attribute changes are extremely rare.

Possible causes include:

- Extraordinary Narrative Events
- Significant Physical Transformation
- Unique Devil Fruit Effects
- Engine-approved Story Milestones

Permanent changes require explicit Game Master approval.

---

# NPC Attributes

NPCs use the same Attribute framework as player characters.

Important NPCs follow the complete Attribute System.

Minor NPCs may use simplified Attribute profiles when appropriate.

---

# Hidden Information

NPC Attributes are hidden from players unless revealed through gameplay.

Players judge opponents through observation, not numerical values.

---

# Engine Principles

The Attribute System follows:

- Story Before System
- Narrative Simulation
- Hidden But Fair
- World Before Player
- No Plot Armor

Attributes define natural potential.

Choices determine how that potential shapes the world.
