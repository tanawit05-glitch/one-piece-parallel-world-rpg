# CHARACTER CREATION SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Character Creation System defines the standardized process for creating a new playable character.

Character creation establishes a character's identity, history, motivations, and starting capabilities while ensuring compatibility with the Narrative Simulation Engine.

Every player character begins as an ordinary individual living within the world, not as a legendary figure.

---

# Design Philosophy

Character creation prioritizes narrative identity over mechanical optimization.

A compelling character is defined by:

- Personality
- Goals
- Relationships
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Life Experience

Statistics exist to support storytelling, not replace it.

---

# Character Creation Timing

New characters are created during:

- Session 0
- Character Replacement
- Approved Story Introduction

Characters are never generated during active scenes unless required by the Game Master.

# Character Concept

The player first defines a simple concept.

Examples:

- Honest Marine Recruit
- Traveling Doctor
- Former Shipwright
- Treasure Hunter
- Wandering Musician
- Island Hunter
- Merchant Bodyguard

The concept guides all later decisions.

---

# Basic Identity

Every character records:

- Name
- Age
- Gender
- Height
- Appearance
- Birth Island
- Native Sea
- Dominant Hand
- Languages
- Distinguishing Features

Identity provides narrative context only unless otherwise specified.

---

# Background

Background represents the character's early life before gameplay.

Background influences:

- Knowledge
- Starting Relationships
- Initial Opportunities
- Narrative Hooks

Background does not guarantee future success.

---

# Occupation

Occupation represents the character's profession before joining the story.

Examples include:

- Fisher
- Cook
- Carpenter
- Sailor
- Doctor
- Hunter
- Merchant
- Guard

Occupation grants appropriate starting experience and skills.

---

# Attributes

Core Attributes define the character's natural capabilities.

Attributes are assigned according to the Attribute System.

No attribute may exceed the starting limits defined by the engine.

---

# Skills

Skills represent learned experience.

Skills originate from:

- Background
- Occupation
- Training
- Life Experience

Skill assignment follows the Skill System.

---

# Traits

Traits describe positive personal qualities.

Examples:

- Calm
- Brave
- Curious
- Patient
- Observant
- Charismatic

Traits influence narrative interpretation and specific mechanics where applicable.

---

# Flaws

Every character must possess meaningful flaws.

Examples:

- Reckless
- Proud
- Impatient
- Gullible
- Cowardly
- Stubborn

Flaws create dramatic opportunities and meaningful decisions.

---

# Dream

Every playable character possesses one major dream.

Examples:

- Discover a legendary island
- Cure every known disease
- Build the strongest ship
- Become the greatest navigator
- Protect a homeland

Dreams provide long-term motivation.

Dreams may evolve through play.

---

# Ambition

Ambition defines the character's immediate direction.

Unlike dreams, ambitions are achievable within shorter timeframes.

Examples:

- Earn enough money for a ship
- Join a famous crew
- Become a Marine Officer
- Learn Haki
- Find a skilled mentor

Ambitions change throughout the campaign.

---

# Relationships

Characters begin with meaningful social connections.

Relationships may include:

- Family
- Friends
- Rivals
- Teachers
- Employers
- Former Crewmates

Relationships exist independently within the world.

---

# Starting Equipment

Characters receive starting equipment based on:

- Occupation
- Background
- Story Circumstances

Equipment is intentionally modest.

Legendary equipment is never available during character creation.

---

# Starting Reputation

New characters begin with an initial reputation determined by their history.

Most characters begin as unknown individuals.

Exceptional backgrounds may grant minor recognition at the Game Master's discretion.

---

# Character Validation

Before approval, every character must satisfy all engine requirements.

Validation confirms:

- Complete Information
- Legal Attribute Allocation
- Legal Skill Allocation
- Valid Background
- Valid Occupation
- Required Trait
- Required Flaw
- Dream Defined
- Ambition Defined

Incomplete characters cannot enter play.

---

# Character Approval

The Game Master performs the final review.

Approval ensures:

- Narrative Consistency
- Mechanical Balance
- World Compatibility

Approved characters officially become part of the living world.

---

# Replacement Characters

Replacement characters follow the same creation process.

Existing world events remain unchanged.

Replacement characters enter the story naturally through narrative circumstances.

---

# Engine Principles

The Character Creation System follows:

- Story Before System
- Narrative Simulation
- Hidden But Fair
- World Before Player
- No Plot Armor

Character creation establishes the beginning of a life within the world, not the beginning of the world itself.
