# RELATIONSHIP SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

The Relationship System defines how characters are connected to other individuals within the Narrative Simulation Engine.

Relationships represent emotional bonds, history, trust, conflict, and shared experience.

They are one of the primary forces shaping character behavior and world interaction.

---

# Design Philosophy

No character exists in isolation.

Every individual is part of a network of connections.

Relationships influence:

- Decisions
- Trust
- Loyalty
- Conflict
- Cooperation
- Emotional responses
- Long-term story development

Relationships are not static statistics.

They are living narrative bonds.

---

# Relationship Definition

A Relationship represents the dynamic connection between two characters.

It is defined by:

- History
- Emotional tone
- Trust level
- Shared experiences
- Current status

Relationships evolve naturally through gameplay.

---

# Core Relationship Types

Relationships are categorized into narrative types rather than numerical labels.

---

## Positive Relationships

Examples:

Friend

Close Friend

Best Friend

Ally

Trusted Companion

Mentor

Student

Family Member

Loyal Crewmate

Positive relationships encourage cooperation and support.

---

## Neutral Relationships

Examples:

Acquaintance

Stranger

Professional Contact

Trade Partner

Neutral relationships are unstable and can evolve in any direction.

---

## Negative Relationships

Examples:

Rival

Enemy

Distrusted Individual

Former Ally

Betrayer

Negative relationships create tension and conflict.

---

## Complex Relationships

Some relationships combine multiple emotional layers.

Examples:

Rival with Respect

Former Friend

Reluctant Ally

Enemy with Shared History

Love-Hate Relationship

These are common in long-term narratives.

---

# Trust

Trust is a core element of relationships.

Trust influences:

- Willingness to cooperate
- Information sharing
- Risk-taking
- Loyalty during conflict

Trust is earned slowly and lost quickly.

---

# History

Shared history defines the depth of a relationship.

Examples:

Saved someone’s life.

Fought together in battle.

Traveled together.

Shared hardship.

Betrayal.

History cannot be ignored in future interactions.

---

# Emotional Tone

Each relationship carries an emotional tone.

Examples:

Warm

Cold

Hostile

Respectful

Distant

Protective

Emotional tone affects dialogue and behavior.

---

# Relationship Development

Relationships evolve through:

Shared experiences.

Conflict resolution.

Cooperation.

Betrayal.

Communication.

Distance or separation.

Absence may weaken relationships over time.

---

# Relationship Decay

Relationships may weaken if:

Characters stop interacting.

Trust is broken.

Goals diverge.

Conflicts remain unresolved.

Decay does not erase history,

but it changes emotional tone.

---

# Relationship Growth

Positive interactions strengthen relationships.

Examples:

Helping in danger.

Keeping promises.

Sharing resources.

Supporting goals.

Protecting each other.

Strong relationships create powerful narrative alliances.

---

# Betrayal

Betrayal permanently alters relationships.

Consequences include:

Loss of trust.

Reputation damage.

Faction consequences.

Emotional trauma.

Some relationships can never fully recover.

---

# Romance

Romantic relationships are possible but not required.

Romance follows the same system rules:

Trust.

History.

Shared experience.

Emotional development.

Romance evolves naturally from interaction,

not from mechanical assignment.

---

# Family Relationships

Family ties are special narrative connections.

They may include:

Parents.

Siblings.

Children.

Guardians.

Family relationships often persist regardless of distance or conflict.

---

# Mentor and Student

Mentorship is a structured relationship based on learning.

Mentors influence:

Skills.

Values.

Career development.

Worldview.

Students may eventually surpass mentors.

---

# Rivalry

Rivalries are competitive relationships.

They may be:

Friendly.

Hostile.

Respectful.

Obsessive.

Rivals often drive personal growth.

---

# Crew Relationships

Crewmates form one of the strongest relationship structures.

Crew bonds include:

Trust.

Shared survival.

Mutual responsibility.

Shared goals.

Crew relationships are central in maritime narratives.

---

# Hidden Relationships

Not all relationships are known immediately.

NPCs may:

Hide intentions.

Fake friendship.

Conceal hatred.

Secretly observe others.

Truth is discovered through interaction.

---

# Public vs Private Relationships

Public perception may differ from reality.

Example:

Two individuals publicly appear as allies but secretly distrust each other.

Or

Enemies publicly but secretly cooperating.

Both layers exist simultaneously.

---

# Relationship Influence on Behavior

Relationships directly affect NPC and character behavior.

Examples:

Trusted allies may provide help without request.

Enemies may act preemptively.

Family may intervene in conflicts.

Rivals may appear at critical moments.

---

# Relationship and Reputation Interaction

Relationships and Reputation are separate systems.

Reputation affects first impressions.

Relationships determine personal behavior.

A famous hero may still be disliked personally.

A feared criminal may have loyal friends.

---

# Relationship and Factions

Factions influence relationships between members.

Examples:

Marines may distrust pirates.

Revolutionaries may oppose government officials.

Merchant guilds may prefer stability.

Faction alignment influences but does not determine relationships.

---

# Game Master Guidelines

The Game Master should use Relationships to:

Create emotional depth.

Drive character decisions.

Generate conflict.

Support long-term storytelling.

Build immersive NPC networks.

Relationships should evolve naturally from gameplay.

Never assign or remove relationships arbitrarily.

---

# Future Expansion

Future systems may introduce:

Crew Bond System.

Family Tree System.

Romance Expansion System.

Faction Diplomacy System.

Political Relationship System.

All expansions must remain compatible with the Core Relationship System.

---

# Engine Principles

The Relationship System follows:

Story Before System.

Narrative Simulation.

Hidden But Fair.

World Before Player.

No Plot Armor.

Every bond is shaped by shared experience.

Every connection tells a story.
