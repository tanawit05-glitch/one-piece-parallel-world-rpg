# RED_LINE

---

# Purpose

`RED_LINE.md` defines the simulation rules governing the Red Line.

The Red Line is the largest geological structure in the world.

It acts as a natural barrier separating the world's oceans while serving as a major political, strategic, and geographical landmark.

This document establishes how the Red Line functions within the world simulation.

---

# Scope

This document defines:

- Red Line Geography
- World Separation
- Surface Accessibility
- Strategic Importance
- Transportation Limitations
- Regional Influence

This document does not define:

- Individual Settlements
- Government Facilities
- Mariejois
- Fish-Man Island
- Reverse Mountain

These are defined in their respective documents.

---

# Core Principle

The Red Line is an impassable continental barrier.

It shapes the world's geography.

It influences politics, trade, military strategy, and exploration.

Its existence affects every sea in the world.

---

# Geological Identity

The Red Line is a massive landmass extending around the entire planet.

Unlike ordinary islands,

it forms a continuous geological barrier.

It permanently separates the world's oceans.

The Red Line cannot be bypassed through ordinary sailing.

---

# Global Separation

The Red Line divides the world's oceans into separate regions.

Travel between major sea regions is heavily restricted.

Movement across the Red Line requires specialized routes or methods defined elsewhere in the Engine.

---

# Accessibility

The majority of the Red Line is inaccessible.

Natural cliffs,

extreme elevation,

and harsh terrain prevent ordinary travel.

Only limited access points exist.

Those access points are defined in future documents.

---

# Strategic Importance

Control of locations connected to the Red Line provides significant strategic advantages.

Possible advantages include:

- Military deployment
- Trade regulation
- Transportation
- Intelligence gathering
- Political influence

The strategic value of Red Line locations should always be considered during world simulation.

---

# Political Influence

The Red Line affects global politics.

Nations,

organizations,

and military forces may compete to secure important routes connected to it.

Political tension surrounding the Red Line may change over time.

---

# Military Importance

The Red Line serves as a natural defensive structure.

Military organizations may use it to:

- Protect territory
- Control movement
- Monitor trade
- Restrict access
- Launch operations

Military presence should vary according to strategic importance.

---

# Economic Influence

Because the Red Line restricts movement,

it naturally influences:

- Trade routes
- Transportation costs
- Supply chains
- Resource distribution
- Regional markets

Changes affecting transportation routes may produce economic consequences.

---

# Environmental Influence

The Red Line influences surrounding environments.

Possible effects include:

- Wind patterns
- Ocean currents
- Migration routes
- Climate interaction

Detailed environmental mechanics are defined in WEATHER.md.

---

# Travel Restrictions

The Red Line cannot be crossed freely.

Players,

NPCs,

merchant vessels,

and military fleets are all subject to the same geographical limitations.

The AI Game Master must apply these restrictions consistently.

---

# World Simulation

The Red Line itself remains geographically stable.

However,

activity surrounding it continuously changes.

Possible ongoing developments include:

- Trade expansion
- Military movement
- Political negotiation
- Criminal operations
- Exploration

---

# AI Game Master Responsibilities

The AI must:

- Preserve geographical consistency.
- Respect movement restrictions.
- Simulate strategic importance.
- Apply identical rules to all characters.

The AI must never:

- Allow unrestricted crossing.
- Ignore geographical barriers.
- Treat the Red Line as ordinary terrain.
- Create unexplained travel shortcuts.
