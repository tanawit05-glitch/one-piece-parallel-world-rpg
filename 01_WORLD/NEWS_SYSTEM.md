# NEWS_SYSTEM

---

# Purpose

`NEWS_SYSTEM.md` defines how information spreads throughout PROJECT ASTER ENGINE.

Information is not shared instantly.

Knowledge travels through realistic communication channels.

Every individual, organization, and nation only knows what they have reasonably learned.

The News System preserves information realism while allowing the world to react naturally to significant events.

---

# Scope

This document defines:

- Information Propagation
- News Sources
- Rumors
- Official Reports
- Public Awareness
- News Reliability
- Information Delay
- Information Persistence

This document does not define:

- Reputation
- Relationships
- World Events
- NPC Memory

Those systems reference this document.

---

# Core Principle

Information has a source.

Information has a path.

Information requires time.

Not everyone knows everything.

Knowledge must spread naturally.

---

# Information Sources

News may originate from multiple sources.

Examples include:

- Eyewitnesses
- Survivors
- Travelers
- Merchants
- Newspapers
- Marine Reports
- Government Announcements
- Den Den Mushi
- Criminal Networks
- Revolutionary Networks

Each source possesses different reliability.

---

# Information Categories

The Engine recognizes several categories of information.

Examples include:

- Local News
- Regional News
- Global News
- Military Reports
- Criminal Activity
- Political Events
- Natural Disasters
- Economic Changes
- Scientific Discoveries

Each category spreads differently.

---

# Information Propagation

News spreads gradually.

Factors affecting propagation include:

- Distance
- Transportation
- Communication methods
- Political censorship
- Public interest
- Reliability of witnesses

Information should never become globally known immediately without justification.

---

# Information Delay

Every piece of information requires time to spread.

Possible delays include:

- Minutes
- Hours
- Days
- Weeks

The delay depends on:

- Event significance
- Communication infrastructure
- Regional accessibility

Timeline consistency must always be maintained.

---

# Rumors

Rumors are unofficial information.

Rumors may be:

- Accurate
- Partially accurate
- Misunderstood
- Exaggerated
- Completely false

Rumors influence public perception regardless of their accuracy.

---

# Official Reports

Official reports originate from recognized authorities.

Examples include:

- Marine Headquarters
- Kingdom Governments
- Local Authorities
- World Government

Official reports are generally more reliable,

but may still contain intentional misinformation.

---

# Newspapers

Newspapers distribute important public information.

Possible contents include:

- Major battles
- Political developments
- Pirate activity
- Bounty updates
- Natural disasters
- Extraordinary discoveries

Publication requires time.

Distribution requires transportation.

---

# Public Awareness

Different populations possess different levels of awareness.

Examples:

A remote village may know nothing about a recent war.

Marine Headquarters may possess classified intelligence.

Pirates may possess underground information before governments.

Knowledge distribution is never perfectly uniform.

---

# Classified Information

Some information remains restricted.

Examples include:

- Military operations
- Intelligence reports
- Secret research
- Hidden facilities
- Political negotiations

Restricted information should only become public through realistic disclosure.

---

# Information Accuracy

Information quality varies.

Possible outcomes include:

- Verified
- Reliable
- Unconfirmed
- Conflicting
- False

Characters should react according to what they believe,

not necessarily objective reality.

---

# Information Persistence

Information becomes part of history.

Historical records may later be referenced by:

- Governments
- Scholars
- Journalists
- Criminal Organizations
- Historians

Information should not disappear without reason.

---

# News Influence

Information influences:

- Reputation
- Public Opinion
- Trade
- Politics
- Crime
- Military Operations
- Diplomatic Relations

The spread of information may generate additional World Events.

---

# AI Game Master Responsibilities

The AI must:

- Spread information realistically.
- Respect communication delays.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Separate rumor from fact.
- Maintain historical consistency.

The AI must never:

- Give universal knowledge.
- Reveal secret information without cause.
- Ignore communication limitations.
- Instantly inform every NPC.

# Design Principles

Knowledge has value.

Information creates influence.

Influence changes behavior.

Behavior changes history.

The world reacts to what people know,

not merely to what actually happened.
