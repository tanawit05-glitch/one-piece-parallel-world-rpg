# GRAND_LINE

---

# Purpose

`GRAND_LINE.md` defines the simulation rules governing the Grand Line.

The Grand Line is the most dangerous and unpredictable maritime region in the world.

Unlike the Four Blues, navigation within the Grand Line follows unique natural laws that significantly affect exploration, travel, combat, logistics, and survival.

This document establishes the rules required for consistent world simulation.

---

# Scope

This document defines:

- Grand Line Environment
- Navigation Rules
- Ocean Characteristics
- Climate Instability
- Travel Conditions
- Regional Simulation
- Environmental Hazards

This document does not define:

- Individual Islands
- Island Weather
- Sea Kings
- Calm Belt
- Red Line

Those systems are defined in their respective documents.

---

# Core Principle

The Grand Line rejects predictability.

Natural laws behave differently from the Four Blues.

Success depends on preparation rather than luck.

Every voyage carries inherent risk.

---

# Geographic Definition

The Grand Line is a continuous ocean belt encircling the world.

It is separated from the Four Blues by the Red Line and the Calm Belt.

Entry into the Grand Line is limited by geography and environmental conditions.

Travel routes are naturally constrained.

---

# Environmental Identity

The Grand Line is characterized by:

- Unstable weather
- Rapid climate shifts
- Irregular sea currents
- Abnormal magnetic fields
- Dangerous wildlife
- Difficult navigation

These conditions exist continuously.

They are fundamental properties of the region.

---

# Magnetic Navigation

Standard navigation methods are unreliable.

Navigation primarily depends on magnetic guidance.

Without a functioning navigation method, determining the correct route becomes extremely difficult.

The exact navigation mechanics are defined in future navigation systems.

---

# Climate Instability

Weather patterns are highly unpredictable.

Multiple climate zones may exist within close proximity.

Possible conditions include:

- Heavy rain
- Extreme heat
- Snowstorms
- Dense fog
- Calm seas
- Violent storms

Environmental changes may occur rapidly.

The AI Game Master should determine weather using the Weather System.

---

# Ocean Conditions

The sea itself presents constant challenges.

Possible hazards include:

- Strong currents
- Whirlpool formation
- Massive waves
- Sudden fog
- Electrical storms
- Reduced visibility

Environmental hazards may exist independently of combat.

---

# Travel Difficulty

Travel through the Grand Line requires significantly greater preparation than travel within the Four Blues.

Factors affecting successful travel include:

- Ship condition
- Crew capability
- Supplies
- Navigation
- Weather
- Sea conditions

Poor preparation greatly increases travel risk.

---

# Island Diversity

Every island within the Grand Line is unique.

Possible differences include:

- Climate
- Culture
- Economy
- Wildlife
- Government
- Technology
- Geography

No standard island template exists.

Each island should be simulated independently.

---

# Isolation

Many islands have limited interaction with neighboring islands.

Trade, culture, and politics may develop independently.

Isolation creates significant regional diversity.

---

# Marine Presence

Marine activity varies throughout the Grand Line.

Some regions are heavily patrolled.

Others receive little governmental attention.

Marine deployment depends on:

- Strategic value
- Crime level
- Political importance
- Trade significance

---

# Pirate Activity

Pirate activity is expected throughout the Grand Line.

The level of activity varies according to:

- Trade routes
- Marine influence
- Local governments
- Available resources
- Territory disputes

Pirate organizations evolve continuously.

---

# Exploration

Exploration is a major component of gameplay.

Players are encouraged to:

- Discover new locations
- Gather information
- Establish relationships
- Trade
- Investigate rumors

Exploration should always involve uncertainty.

Unknown locations should never be fully described before discovery.

---

# Risk and Reward

The Grand Line offers greater opportunities than the Four Blues.

Possible rewards include:

- Rare resources
- Valuable information
- Powerful allies
- Wealth
- Influence

Greater rewards are balanced by significantly greater risks.

---

# World Simulation

The Grand Line continues to evolve regardless of player involvement.

Possible ongoing changes include:

- Political conflict
- Pirate expansion
- Economic shifts
- Island development
- Natural disasters
- Trade route changes

Simulation never pauses.

---

# AI Game Master Responsibilities

The AI must:

- Maintain environmental unpredictability.
- Preserve geographical consistency.
- Simulate regional development.
- Apply realistic travel challenges.
- Respect all established world rules.

The AI must never:

- Make travel completely safe.
- Ignore environmental hazards.
- Simplify navigation without reason.
- Treat every island as identical.

Navigation is meaningful.
