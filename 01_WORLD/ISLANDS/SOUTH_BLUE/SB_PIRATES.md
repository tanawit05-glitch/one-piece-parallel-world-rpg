# SOUTH BLUE PIRATES SYSTEM

World Type: One Piece Parallel World

---

# OVERVIEW

Piracy in South Blue is not a single unified culture.

Instead, it is divided into **4 Pirate Archetypes**, shaped by geography, economy, and anomaly zones.

Unlike East Blue pirates (mostly small crews), South Blue pirates include:

- Organized syndicates
- Research-based crews
- Speed/navigation specialists
- Mutation survivors

---

# PIRATE CLASSIFICATIONS

## 1. ⚓ TRADITIONAL PIRATES
(Common in Sunburst / Aurora / Coralhaven routes)

- Crew size: 10–40
- Focus: Raiding, treasure hunting
- Weak against Marines

---

## 2. ☠️ SYNDICATE PIRATES
(Red Lagoon influence)

- Crew size: 50–300 (network-based)
- Structure: Mafia-style hierarchy
- Activities:
  - Smuggling
  - Assassination contracts
  - Illegal auctions
- Extremely organized

---

## 3. 🧭 EXPLORER PIRATES
(Torino / Sandglass Key influence)

- Focus: Discovery, ruins, forbidden zones
- Often not hostile unless provoked
- May cooperate with scholars or governments

---

## 4. ⚡ FAST-ROUTE PIRATES
(Windsong Isle influence)

- Specialize in high-speed ships
- Hit-and-run tactics
- Difficult to capture
- Often act as smugglers or escorts

---

## 5. 🧬 MUTATION SURVIVOR PIRATES
(Blood Mangrove influence)

- Physically altered crews
- Unstable abilities
- Often unpredictable behavior
- Sometimes non-human intelligence

# PIRATE ECONOMY ROLE

Pirates are not just fighters:

- Smuggling transporters
- Information brokers
- Illegal explorers
- Syndicate enforcers
- Navigation specialists

---

# RELATIONSHIP WITH MARINES

## Peace Zones
- Capture & release possible
- Negotiation exists

## Trade Zones
- Active pursuit
- Escort conflicts common

## Lawless Zones
- Open warfare possible

## Anomaly Zones
- Containment priority over capture

---

# MAJOR PIRATE BEHAVIOR PATTERNS

- Avoid Aurora Port direct conflict
- Use Windsong Isle for fast escape routes
- Hide in Coralhaven underwater tunnels
- Trade through Red Lagoon networks
- Investigate Sandglass Key rumors

---

# GM NOTES

South Blue pirates are designed to be:

- More “system-based” than brute force
- Integrated into economy and navigation systems
- Capable of becoming allies or enemies depending on arc

This creates:
👉 Dynamic faction storytelling instead of static enemies
