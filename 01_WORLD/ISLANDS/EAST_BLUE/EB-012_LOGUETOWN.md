# OVERVIEW

Loguetown is the largest commercial city in East Blue and serves as the primary gateway to Reverse Mountain and the Grand Line. Nearly every captain sailing toward the Grand Line eventually visits Loguetown to resupply, recruit crew members, repair ships, and prepare for the dangerous voyage ahead.

Because of its strategic location, Loguetown maintains one of East Blue's strongest Marine presences. Merchants, bounty hunters, pirates, craftsmen, scholars, and travelers from every Blue can be found within its busy streets.

Although considered one of the safest major cities in East Blue, criminal organizations constantly attempt to exploit its enormous flow of commerce.

# CULTURE

## General Culture

Loguetown is often called **"The City of Beginnings and Endings."**

For many people, it is the final destination of a peaceful life.

For adventurers, pirates, Marines, and merchants, it is the first step toward the Grand Line.

Because thousands of travelers arrive every month, the city has developed one of the most diverse cultures in East Blue.

Residents are accustomed to meeting people from every sea and generally judge others by their actions rather than their origins.

---

## Hospitality

Visitors are welcomed regardless of occupation, provided they obey local laws.

Restaurants, inns, taverns, and bathhouses remain open late into the night due to the constant flow of travelers.

## GM Guidance

Loguetown should feel like a living metropolis where hundreds of independent stories unfold every day.

Merchant prices, Marine patrols, public opinion, criminal activity, and trade volume should evolve naturally in response to player actions.

Because it is the final major stop before the Grand Line, Loguetown is an excellent location for:

- Recruiting crew members
- Purchasing equipment
- Gathering intelligence
- Introducing rival crews
- Beginning long-term story arcs
- Establishing recurring NPCs

Player choices made here can have lasting consequences that echo throughout the rest of the campaign.
