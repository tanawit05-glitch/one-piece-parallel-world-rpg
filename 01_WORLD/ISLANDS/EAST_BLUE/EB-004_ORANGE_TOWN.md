# OVERVIEW

Orange Town is one of East Blue's oldest commercial port cities and serves as an important regional trading center connecting numerous nearby islands.

Unlike heavily fortified government cities, Orange Town developed through commerce rather than military expansion. Its streets are densely packed with shops, workshops, warehouses, inns, restaurants and merchant offices, making it one of the busiest civilian settlements in the sea.

Because thousands of travelers pass through every month, Orange Town has become a melting pot of cultures, professions and information.

Although generally peaceful, the constant flow of money also attracts smugglers, thieves and organized criminals.

---

# HISTORY

## Early Settlement

The first settlers established Orange Town nearly two centuries ago around a naturally protected harbor.

Fishing and coastal trade quickly transformed the settlement into an important marketplace.

---

## Merchant Expansion

As maritime trade throughout East Blue increased, merchant families invested heavily in warehouses, docks and public infrastructure.

Roads were expanded.

New districts emerged.

Population steadily increased.

---

## Modern Era

Orange Town now serves as one of East Blue's busiest civilian ports.

Merchant vessels arrive almost daily carrying food, timber, ore, textiles and manufactured goods.

Rather than depending upon one major industry, Orange Town prospers through diversified commerce.
