# OVERVIEW

Shells Town is a fortified port city located along one of East Blue's busiest maritime routes.

Unlike Dawn Island, whose economy is supported by agriculture and natural resources, Shells Town exists primarily because of its military importance.

The city developed around a Marine Base that gradually attracted merchants, craftsmen, shipbuilders and travelers seeking protection from piracy.

Today it serves as one of the strongest Marine-controlled ports in East Blue.

Its streets are orderly, commerce is active, and law enforcement is strict.

However, beneath its disciplined appearance, political tension, organized crime and corruption continue to threaten the city's stability.

# HISTORY

Originally established as a small fishing settlement.

After repeated pirate attacks, the World Government constructed a Marine fortress overlooking the harbor.

Merchants quickly settled nearby due to increased security.

Within several decades Shells Town became one of East Blue's largest military ports.

Today nearly every aspect of the city's economy depends on maritime trade and Marine logistics.

# GOVERNMENT

## Administrative Structure

Shells Town is governed jointly by the Town Council and the Marine Regional Headquarters.

While civilian matters remain under local jurisdiction, military affairs are controlled entirely by the Marines.

The cooperation between both institutions has transformed Shells Town into one of East Blue's safest ports.

# GM NOTES

Recommended Campaign Length

4–10 Sessions

Recommended Difficulty

Beginner to Intermediate

Core Themes

- Justice
- Duty
- Corruption
- Military Politics
- Trade
- Smuggling
- Investigation

Shells Town should feel fundamentally different from Dawn Island.

Where Dawn Island focuses on exploration and social conflict, Shells Town emphasizes law enforcement, organized crime, and military influence.

Players should constantly feel that Marines are present.

Their actions should influence patrol frequency, public trust, merchant confidence, and criminal activity.
