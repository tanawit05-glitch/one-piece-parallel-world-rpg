# OVERVIEW

Dawn Island is one of the largest inhabited islands in East Blue and serves as a major political, agricultural, and military center within the region. Unlike many islands that rely on a single settlement, Dawn Island supports multiple communities connected through established roads, rivers, and maritime trade.

The island is divided into three major regions:

- Goa Kingdom
- Mt. Colubo Region
- Gray Terminal

Each region possesses its own culture, economy, and security concerns, creating opportunities for both cooperation and conflict.

Although regarded as one of the safest islands in East Blue, the contrast between prosperity and poverty has shaped Dawn Island into a land where corruption, ambition, and justice constantly collide.

# GOVERNMENT

## Political System

Goa Kingdom is a hereditary constitutional monarchy recognized by the World Government.

While the royal family acts as the symbolic head of state, most administrative affairs are handled by appointed ministers and regional governors.

The kingdom values stability, trade, and cooperation with Marine authorities.

---

## Royal Family

The current royal family has ruled Dawn Island for over two centuries.

Unlike many kingdoms, succession is overseen by the Royal Council to reduce internal conflict.

Royal responsibilities include:

- Foreign diplomacy
- Military authorization
- Appointment of ministers
- Emergency declarations
- Approval of national infrastructure projects

---

# CULTURE

## General Culture

The people of Dawn Island value hard work, honor, education, and community cooperation.

Unlike many islands dominated by commerce alone, Dawn Island maintains a balance between agriculture, craftsmanship, military service, and scholarship.

Respect for family and local traditions is deeply rooted throughout every settlement.

Children commonly learn practical skills from an early age, including fishing, farming, navigation, carpentry, or trade.

---

## Hospitality

Visitors are generally welcomed warmly.

Travelers who respect local customs often receive assistance from villagers, while criminals quickly attract the attention of both civilians and the Kingdom Guard.

---

## Social Classes
