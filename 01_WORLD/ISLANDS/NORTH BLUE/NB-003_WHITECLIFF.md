# OVERVIEW

Whitecliff is a massive coastal island dominated by towering white limestone cliffs that encircle nearly the entire landmass.

The island is notorious among sailors as one of the most dangerous navigation zones in North Blue due to unpredictable winds, vertical currents, and unstable cliff collapses that can destroy ships before they reach shore.

Most ships avoid the island entirely unless forced by storms or navigation errors.

## STRUCTURE OF THE ISLAND

Whitecliff is divided into 3 major layers:

### 1. Outer Cliff Ring

- 300–800 meter vertical cliffs
- Constant erosion from sea wind
- Falling rock zones

### 2. Mid Slope Forest

- Dense forest growing on unstable terrain
- Hidden caves and sinkholes
- Limited safe paths

### 3. Inner Basin

- Calm valley-like zone
- Small freshwater lake
- Ancient ruins scattered
