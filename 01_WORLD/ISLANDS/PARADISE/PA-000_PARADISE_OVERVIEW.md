# PARADISE (GRAND LINE – FIRST HALF)

## 🌊 Overview
Paradise is the first half of the Grand Line, a sea defined by unstable physics, extreme weather, and fragmented geopolitical control.

Unlike East Blue, Paradise is not a linear world.
It is a **network of islands (Island Nodes)** connected by probability-based navigation.

---

## 🧭 Core World Rule

Travel is not fixed.

Instead, movement is determined by:

- Log Pose synchronization
- Magnetic field shifts between islands
- Weather distortion zones
- Faction interference (Marines / Pirates / Revolutionaries)
- Narrative pressure (GM-driven world response)

---

## 🧠 World Structure (Paradise Layer Model)

Paradise is divided into 5 macro-layers:

### 1. 🚪 Entry Layer
- Reverse Mountain
- First exposure to Grand Line instability

### 2. ⚔️ Survival Layer
- Whisky Peak
- Little Garden
- Drum Island
> Focus: survival, resources, first conflicts

### 3. 👑 Kingdom Layer
- Alabasta
> Focus: political war, civil conflict, large-scale stakes

### 4. ⬆️ Vertical Layer
- Jaya
- Skypeia (Sky Route)
> Focus: unknown physics, vertical navigation, myth layers

### 5. 🏙️ Civilization Collapse Layer
- Water 7
- Enies Lobby
- Thriller Bark
- Sabaody Archipelago
> Focus: world system pressure, government escalation

---

## 🌐 Island System Rule

Each island is treated as a **NODE**, not a location.

Every node contains:

- Entry conditions
- Exit conditions
- Faction influence level
- Hidden route triggers
- Log Pose stability rating

---

## ⚖️ World Behavior Rule

The world reacts dynamically to:

- Player reputation
- Marine activity level
- Pirate expansion
- Trade disruption
- Major story events

---

## 🎯 GM Principle

Paradise must feel like:

> “A living system that moves even when players do nothing.”

- Marines patrol and shift routes
- Pirates relocate based on pressure
- Trade routes collapse or evolve
- Islands change importance over time

---

## 🧩 Design Philosophy

- No fixed progression path
- No guaranteed next island
- Exploration is probabilistic
- Narrative drives geography

---

## 🔥 Core Identity of Paradise

Paradise is not a map.

Paradise is a **living network of unstable worlds.**
