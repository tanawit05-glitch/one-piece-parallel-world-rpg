# OVERVIEW

Thriller Bark is not a natural island.

It is an enormous mobile fortress constructed upon one of the largest ships ever built, drifting silently through the mysterious waters of the Florian Triangle.

Unlike ordinary islands, Thriller Bark constantly changes its position, making navigation nearly impossible without experienced navigators or exceptional luck.

The island has earned a terrifying reputation throughout Paradise.

Merchant crews avoid it.

Marine patrols rarely enter its surrounding waters.

Even experienced pirates often choose longer routes rather than risk sailing through the perpetual darkness surrounding the island.

Although countless ghost stories circulate across the Grand Line, very few people truly know what exists within Thriller Bark.

Many ships enter.

Few are ever seen again.

# MAJOR REGIONS

## Haunted Forest

A dense woodland where towering dead trees block nearly all sunlight.

Visibility rarely exceeds a few dozen meters.

Travelers frequently report hearing voices despite seeing no one nearby.

---

## Central Mansion

An enormous gothic manor overlooking the island.

Built centuries ago using dark stone and reinforced timber.

Its countless halls, towers and underground chambers remain largely unexplored.

---

## Cemetery Fields

Thousands of graves stretch across rolling hills.

Many monuments have become impossible to identify due to age and neglect.

Some graves appear far newer than they should.

---

## Forgotten Village

An abandoned settlement slowly reclaimed by nature.

Buildings remain structurally intact despite decades of apparent abandonment.

Doors occasionally open without explanation.

---

## Black Swamp

A marshland filled with poisonous plants and unstable ground.

Travel becomes extremely dangerous after rainfall.

---

## Western Cliffs

Jagged cliffs overlooking the Florian Triangle.

Violent waves constantly batter the shoreline.

Hidden sea caves exist beneath the rock formations.

---

## Old Shipyard

The remains of an enormous dockyard once used to repair vessels.

Rusted cranes and broken dry docks dominate the landscape.

Many underground workshops remain sealed.

---

# NATURAL PHENOMENA

## Eternal Fog

A supernatural fog surrounds Thriller Bark throughout the year.

Visibility may suddenly fall below five meters.

Sound travels strangely through the mist.

---

## Ghost Lights

Small floating lights appear after sunset.

Most disappear when approached.

Some appear to guide travelers.

Others lead them deeper into danger.

---

## Shadow Mist

Certain areas become unnaturally cold.

Shadows seem darker than normal.

## Midnight Bell

On rare nights, enormous bells echo across the island despite no visible bell towers.

No official explanation exists.

# CULTURE

## General Culture

Life on Thriller Bark is defined by resilience.

Generations of isolation have taught its people to value practical skills above wealth or social status.

Every resident learns basic survival, navigation and emergency medicine from childhood.

Stories, ghost legends and maritime folklore are deeply woven into everyday life.

Rather than fearing the island itself, locals respect it.

The greatest mistake, they believe, is becoming overconfident.

# SECRETS

Historical evidence suggests Thriller Bark has been expanded repeatedly across several centuries.

Large portions of the fortress were built upon structures even older than the ship itself.

Several forgotten tunnels connect distant regions of the island through hidden underground passages.

Ancient journals describe expeditions into the Florian Triangle that encountered phenomena still unexplained today.

Many recovered artifacts originate from ships that officially never existed.

Certain sections of the Central Mansion remain sealed by mechanisms no modern engineer understands.

# GM NOTES

Recommended Campaign Length

8–14 Sessions

Recommended Player Level

Mid Paradise

Core Themes

- Gothic Exploration
- Survival
- Mystery
- Horror
- Salvage
- Ancient Ruins
- Isolation
- Maritime Legends

Thriller Bark should feel fundamentally different from every other island in Paradise.

Silence, darkness and uncertainty should be constant companions.

The greatest threats are often psychological as much as physical.

Allow players to question whether strange events are caused by natural phenomena, forgotten technology or simple superstition.

Player decisions may permanently affect

- Settlement Safety
- Salvage Operations
- Public Morale
- Trade Routes
- Exploration Progress
- Crime Activity
- Local Reputation
- Infrastructure Recovery

Every journey through Thriller Bark should leave players wondering whether they truly uncovered the island's secrets—or merely scratched the surface.
