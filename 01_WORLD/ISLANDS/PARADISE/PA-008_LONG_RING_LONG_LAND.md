# OVERVIEW

Long Ring Long Land is a unique archipelago composed of numerous small islands connected only during periods of extremely low tide.

The islands are famous for their endless grasslands, gentle climate, and unusual wildlife whose bodies have adapted over countless generations to the island's extraordinary environment.

Because the sea periodically recedes, local residents traditionally travel between islands on foot, allowing trade, festivals and cultural exchange to flourish.

Although peaceful compared to many Grand Line islands, travelers should never underestimate the dangers of sudden tidal changes, wild animals and isolated grassland regions.
