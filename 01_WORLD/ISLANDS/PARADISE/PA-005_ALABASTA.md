# OVERVIEW

Alabasta Kingdom is one of the oldest and largest nations affiliated with the World Government. Situated within Paradise, the kingdom spans an enormous desert continent where only a handful of fertile regions support permanent settlements.

Despite its harsh climate, Alabasta has flourished through centuries of engineering, trade, agriculture, and river management. Cities have been constructed around rivers, underground reservoirs, and ancient oasis systems, allowing civilization to thrive where survival should have been impossible.

Caravan routes connect distant settlements across endless dunes, while merchant ships arrive daily through western ports carrying goods from every corner of Paradise.

The kingdom is respected for its political stability, military discipline, and rich history, though maintaining order across such a vast desert remains a constant challenge.

Travelers often underestimate the dangers beyond the city walls. Sandstorms, giant wildlife, bandits, dehydration, and the immense scale of the desert claim countless lives every year.

# DESERT ECOSYSTEM

Although often described as empty, Alabasta's desert supports an enormous variety of life specially adapted to extreme conditions.

Water determines every aspect of survival.

Most wildlife becomes active after sunset.

Large predators remain hidden beneath sand until prey approaches.

Travelers rarely move during midday unless absolutely necessary.

Experienced caravan leaders navigate using stars, wind direction, rock formations, and ancient road markers rather than relying solely on maps.

---

# NATURAL PHENOMENA

## Great Sandstorms

Massive storms capable of reducing visibility to only a few meters.

Entire dunes may shift overnight.

Old ruins are frequently buried or uncovered.

---

## Mirage Fields

Extreme heat produces convincing optical illusions.

Inexperienced travelers often mistake mirages for real settlements.

---

## Singing Dunes

Certain dunes emit deep humming sounds when wind passes across their surfaces.

Local legends claim the sounds are voices from forgotten civilizations.

---

## Oasis Bloom

Following rare rainfall, dormant desert plants bloom simultaneously across hundreds of kilometers, transforming portions of the desert into fields of flowers for only a few days.

The Royal Family serves as the head of state, while day-to-day administration is handled by ministers, provincial governors, military commanders, and civil officials.

The kingdom has maintained diplomatic relations with neighboring nations for centuries and is regarded as one of the most politically stable kingdoms within Paradise.

- Agriculture

# CULTURE

## General Culture

The people of Alabasta have adapted to one of the harshest environments in Paradise.

Life in the desert has taught generations that cooperation is more valuable than wealth, and that water is more precious than gold.

Hospitality is considered a sacred tradition. Refusing water to a traveler in genuine need is regarded as one of the gravest social disgraces.

Communities celebrate endurance, discipline, craftsmanship, and loyalty to one's family and hometown.

---

## Hospitality

Guests are traditionally welcomed with:

- Fresh Water
- Date Fruits
- Herbal Tea
- Shade
- Warm Meals

Foreign merchants are generally treated with respect, provided they obey local laws and customs.

# SECRETS

The kingdom possesses engineering records describing underground water systems that no longer appear on modern maps.

Several ancient ruins were intentionally sealed by previous generations to prevent dangerous discoveries from becoming public.

Not every archaeological expedition reported to the government is officially documented.

Certain merchant families secretly finance illegal excavations throughout the desert.

Some underground chambers contain construction techniques that modern engineers still cannot reproduce.

The deepest desert continues to reveal forgotten structures after major sandstorms, suggesting that much of Alabasta's true history remains buried.

Several remote oasis communities preserve oral traditions describing civilizations older than the current kingdom.

Recommended Campaign Length

10–20 Sessions

Mid Paradise

Core Themes

- Survival
- Exploration
- Politics
- Ancient History
- Desert Adventure
- Trade
- Engineering
- Justice

Alabasta should feel enormous.

Travel between cities should require planning, supplies and reliable navigation.

Every settlement should possess its own economy, traditions, problems and influential figures.

Water should be treated as one of the kingdom's most valuable strategic resources.

Player decisions may permanently affect:

- Caravan Safety
- Trade Prices
- Public Opinion
- Water Distribution
- Political Stability
- Merchant Relationships
- Crime Rates
- Military Readiness
- Archaeological Discoveries

Every journey across the desert should remind players that the environment itself is one of the greatest challenges in the Grand Line.
