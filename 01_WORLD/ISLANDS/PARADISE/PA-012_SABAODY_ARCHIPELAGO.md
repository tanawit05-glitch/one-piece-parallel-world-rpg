# OVERVIEW

Sabaody Archipelago is the final great crossroads of Paradise and the last major civilization before travelers attempt to enter the New World.

Rather than a single island, Sabaody consists of dozens of interconnected mangrove groves whose enormous roots rise above the sea, creating natural islands.

Every district is built upon the roots of giant Yarukiman Mangrove trees.

These trees continuously produce natural bubbles, allowing unique transportation, architecture, industry and technology unlike anywhere else in the world.

Because every route toward the New World passes nearby, Sabaody attracts nearly every kind of traveler imaginable.

Pirates.

Marines.

Merchants.

Treasure Hunters.

Scientists.

Slave Traders.

World Government Officials.

Foreign Nobles.

Shipwrights.

Bounty Hunters.

Information Brokers.

Criminal Syndicates.

Every day thousands of ships arrive.

Some continue toward the New World.

Many never leave.

# THE YARUKIMAN MANGROVES

The entire archipelago exists because of enormous Yarukiman Mangrove trees.

Their gigantic roots emerge from the ocean floor and intertwine to create natural islands.

Every Grove is connected through these root systems.

The trees naturally generate bubbles from their resin.

These bubbles have become the foundation of local civilization.

## Bubble Rain

During certain humid mornings millions of tiny bubbles drift across the archipelago.

Visibility decreases.

---

## Root Growth

The mangrove roots slowly expand every year.

Entire districts occasionally require rebuilding.

Roads shift naturally over decades.

---

## Bubble Storm

Rare weather conditions cause unstable bubbles to explode throughout parts of the city.

Transportation temporarily shuts down.

---

## Giant Root Tremors

Massive underwater root movement occasionally causes localized earthquakes.

Buildings are specifically designed to withstand these events.
