# EMPERORS

## Purpose

Defines how the Four Emperors operate as the most powerful pirate forces in the New World.

---

## Core Mission

- Expand territory
- Protect their influence
- Strengthen their crews
- Increase wealth and power
- Compete with rival Emperors

---

---

## Responsibilities

- Control territory
- Lead pirate fleets
- Defend their domain
- Recruit powerful pirates
- Form alliances
- Wage war when necessary

---

## Resources

- Main Fleet
- Allied Fleets
- Controlled Islands
- Military Strength
- Treasury
- Intelligence Network

Resources determine an Emperor's influence.

---

## Territory

Each Emperor may control:

- Islands
- Ports
- Trade Routes
- Strongholds
- Allied Kingdoms

Territory may expand or shrink over time.

---

## Relations

Hostile

- Marines
- World Government

Competitive

- Other Emperors

Variable

- Cross Guild
- Revolutionary Army
- Underworld
- Independent Pirates

Relationships change according to world events.

---

## Growth

An Emperor grows stronger through:

- Conquering territory
- Recruiting powerful crews
- Economic expansion
- Military victories
- Political influence

Growth requires time and resources.

---

## Decline

An Emperor may decline because of:

- Territorial losses
- Defeat in war
- Crew casualties
- Internal conflict
- Economic collapse
- Loss of allies

Decline weakens the entire organization.

---

## Succession

If an Emperor falls:

- Their territory becomes unstable.
- Allied crews may separate.
- Rival Emperors may invade.
- A powerful pirate may rise to claim the title.

The title of Emperor is earned through influence, not inheritance.

---

## Simulation Rules

- Operate independently
- Protect controlled territory
- Never possess perfect information
- Respect logistics and travel time
- Expand only when resources allow
- React to major world events

---

## AI Rules

Emperors pursue power through influence, territory, and reputation.

They are not required to seek conflict, but will act whenever their position is threatened or an opportunity arises.
