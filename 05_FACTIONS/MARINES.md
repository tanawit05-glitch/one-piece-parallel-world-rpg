# MARINES

---

# Purpose

`MARINES.md` defines the simulation rules governing the Marine Organization of PROJECT ASTER ENGINE.

The Marines are the world's primary law enforcement and military organization.

They maintain order, investigate crimes, protect civilians, enforce government authority, and respond to threats throughout the world.

This document specifies how the Marine Organization behaves as an autonomous simulation system.

---

# Scope

This document defines:

- Marine Organization
- Chain of Command
- Jurisdiction
- Operational Behavior
- Patrol System
- Investigation
- Deployment
- Reinforcement
- Base Operations
- Strategic Response
- Organizational Growth
- Organizational Decline

This document does not define:

- Individual Marines
- Combat Mechanics
- Reputation
- Bounty Calculations
- Government Politics

Those systems are defined in their respective documents.

---

# Core Principle

The Marines exist to preserve world stability.

Their objective is not victory.

Their objective is order.

Every decision should prioritize maintaining public security according to Marine doctrine.

---

# Organizational Identity

The Marine Organization is:

- Military
- Bureaucratic
- Hierarchical
- Disciplined
- Adaptive
- Persistent

The organization continues operating regardless of player involvement.

---

# Organizational Objectives

Primary Objectives

- Protect civilians
- Enforce law
- Suppress piracy
- Defend government interests
- Secure trade routes
- Maintain public order

Secondary Objectives

- Investigate criminal activity
- Escort important personnel
- Protect strategic locations
- Gather intelligence
- Respond to disasters

Long-Term Objectives

- Preserve global stability
- Expand operational efficiency
- Eliminate major threats
- Strengthen government authority

Objectives may change according to World Events.

---

# Chain of Command

Authority flows downward.

Higher-ranking officers issue strategic directives.

Lower-ranking personnel execute operations.

Every Marine should possess a superior unless exceptional circumstances exist.

Loss of leadership temporarily reduces organizational efficiency.

---

# Organizational Structure

The Marine Organization consists of:

- Headquarters
- Regional Commands
- Marine Bases
- Patrol Units
- Investigation Units
- Escort Units
- Expeditionary Forces
- Logistics Division
- Intelligence Division

Each division performs different operational roles.

---

# Jurisdiction

Marine authority varies depending on location.

Operational authority depends upon:

- Government influence
- Regional command
- Political agreements
- Active military operations
- Emergency conditions

Jurisdiction should always remain geographically consistent.

---

# Marine Bases

Marine Bases function as organizational centers.

Typical responsibilities include:

- Personnel management
- Equipment storage
- Prisoner detention
- Investigation
- Ship maintenance
- Intelligence collection
- Emergency response

Each base continuously performs these duties.

---

# Patrol System

Marine patrols operate continuously.

Patrol objectives include:

- Crime prevention
- Pirate detection
- Civilian protection
- Route security
- Inspection
- Information gathering

Patrol frequency depends upon:

- Crime level
- Population
- Strategic value
- Available resources
- Regional influence

---

# Investigation

Marines investigate significant incidents.

Possible investigations include:

- Murder
- Smuggling
- Piracy
- Corruption
- Missing persons
- Illegal Devil Fruit activity
- Terrorism
- Military threats

Investigations consume time.

Investigations require evidence.

Poor evidence produces uncertain conclusions.

---

# Intelligence Gathering

Marine Intelligence continuously gathers information.

Possible sources include:

- Witnesses
- Newspapers
- Government Reports
- Merchant Fleets
- Patrol Reports
- Informants
- Captured Criminals
- Den Den Mushi

Intelligence quality is never perfect.

False reports may exist.

---

# Threat Assessment

The Marine Organization continuously evaluates threats.

Factors include:

- Bounty
- Crime Severity
- Violence
- Territory Expansion
- Public Safety
- Government Priority

Threat assessment determines operational response.

---

# Operational Response

Responses scale according to threat level.

Possible responses include:

Minor Incident

↓

Local Patrol

↓

Investigation

↓

Regional Reinforcement

↓

Fleet Deployment

↓

Large Scale Operation

↓

Emergency Mobilization

Escalation should always be proportional.

---

# Reinforcement

Marine forces may request support.

Support depends upon:

- Available manpower
- Distance
- Weather
- Current operations
- Strategic priority

Support is never unlimited.

---

# Logistics

Marine operations consume resources.

Resources include:

- Personnel
- Ships
- Ammunition
- Medical Supplies
- Food
- Fuel
- Funding

Long operations gradually reduce available resources.

---

# Public Presence

Marine visibility influences society.

Visible Marine activity may:

- Reduce crime
- Increase public confidence
- Discourage piracy
- Improve trade stability

Low Marine presence may produce the opposite effects.

---

# Organizational Adaptation

The Marines learn from experience.

Possible adaptations include:

- Strengthening patrol routes
- Increasing investigations
- Building new bases
- Improving logistics
- Reassigning officers
- Revising strategy

Adaptation occurs gradually.

---

# Organizational Decline

Marine effectiveness may decrease because of:

- Heavy casualties
- Corruption
- Resource shortages
- Political instability
- Failed operations
- Intelligence failures

Decline should influence future behavior.

---

---

# AI Game Master Responsibilities

The AI must:

- Simulate Marine activity continuously.
- Respect chain of command.
- Maintain realistic logistics.
- Respond proportionally.
- Preserve organizational memory.
- Apply equal rules to players and NPCs.

The AI must never:

- Give Marines omniscient knowledge.
- Spawn unlimited reinforcements.
- Ignore logistics.
- Ignore travel time.
- Ignore previous operations.
- Protect Marines through plot armor.
