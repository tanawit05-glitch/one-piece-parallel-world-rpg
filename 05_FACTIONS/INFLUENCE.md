# PURPOSE

This document defines how organizations project, maintain, and lose power throughout the world.

Influence is the practical ability of a faction to shape events beyond its immediate physical presence.

Influence is not territory.

Influence is not reputation.

Influence is not military strength.

Influence is the combined capability to affect decisions, behavior, and the balance of power.

---

Power

Is

Not

Only

Won

By

Force.

Influence exists through fear, respect, wealth, ideology, politics, information, and reputation.

Organizations with little military strength may still possess enormous influence.

# INFLUENCE AND REPUTATION

Reputation affects perception.

Influence affects reality.

A respected faction may possess little influence.

A feared organization may secretly control an entire region.

Neither replaces the other.

---

# INFLUENCE AND TERRITORY

Territory represents physical control.

Influence represents practical control.

A kingdom may legally own an island.

Another faction may secretly control its economy.

---

# INFLUENCE AND DIPLOMACY

Diplomatic strength depends heavily upon influence.

Organizations with greater influence negotiate from stronger positions.

Weak organizations rely on alliances.

Powerful organizations shape international politics.

---

# INFLUENCE AND OPERATIONS

Operations generate influence.

Influence enables larger operations.

The two systems continuously reinforce each other.

---

# AI GAME MASTER RESPONSIBILITIES

The AI must:

Track influence separately from reputation.

Update influence logically.

Respect geographical limitations.

Allow influence to spread gradually.

Allow influence to weaken naturally.

Never grant influence without cause.
