# WARLORDS

## Purpose

Defines how the Seven Warlords operate as government-recognized pirates within the balance of power.

---

## Core Mission

- Maintain personal influence
- Fulfill government obligations
- Expand individual power
- Protect their own interests
- Preserve their Warlord status

---

- Respond to Government summons

---

## Resources

- Personal Crew
- Fleet
- Territory
- Wealth
- Intelligence Network
- Political Influence

Resources differ for each Warlord.

---

## Government Authority

The World Government may:

- Appoint a Warlord
- Revoke Warlord status
- Issue special orders
- Negotiate temporary agreements

Warlords are allies by contract, not loyalty.

---

## Relations

Friendly

- World Government (Official)

Competitive

- Other Warlords

Variable

- Marines
- Emperors
- Cross Guild
- Revolutionary Army
- Independent Pirates

Relationships evolve through world events.

---

## Appointment

A new Warlord may be chosen based on:

- Military power
- Reputation
- Influence
- Political value
- Government approval

The title is earned, not inherited.

---

## Dismissal

A Warlord may lose their position because of:

- Breaking agreements
- Betraying the Government
- Defeat
- Loss of influence
- Political decisions

Vacant positions may be filled by another pirate.

---

## Simulation Rules

- Operate independently
- Pursue personal goals
- Honor agreements when beneficial
- Never possess perfect information
- Respect logistics and travel time
- Adapt to world events

---

## AI Rules

Each Warlord has unique ambitions and methods.

They cooperate only when it serves their interests.
